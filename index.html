From Our Team

Cleaning Tips & Tricks

Pro insights from Michel & Evelyn — simple habits that keep your home looking freshly cleaned between visits.

🧹
Kitchen

Clean Your Microwave in 5 Minutes — No Scrubbing

Fill a microwave-safe bowl with 1 cup of water and a few slices of lemon. Heat on high for 3 minutes, then let it sit for 2 more. The steam loosens every splatter — just wipe clean with a cloth. No chemicals, no elbow grease.

MU
Michel Uzhca · Pro Tip
🚿
Bathroom

Prevent Soap Scum Before It Starts

After your shower, do a quick 20-second squeegee on the glass doors and tile walls. This single habit removes 80% of the water that causes soap scum and hard water stains. Pair it with a weekly spray of white vinegar and water for a glass that stays crystal clear.

EL
Evelyn Loja · Pro Tip
🛋️
Living Room

The Top-to-Bottom Rule That Saves You Time

Always clean from the highest point in a room down to the floor. Dust ceiling fans and shelves first, then wipe surfaces, and vacuum last. If you vacuum before dusting, you'll just be pushing dirt back onto clean floors. This one rule cuts your cleaning time in half.

MU
Michel Uzhca · Pro Tip
🪟
Windows

Streak-Free Windows Every Time

Skip paper towels — they leave lint and streaks. Use a microfiber cloth or a clean squeegee with a mix of 1 part white vinegar to 4 parts water. Clean windows on a cloudy day, not in direct sunlight — the sun dries the solution too fast and streaks form before you can wipe.

EL
Evelyn Loja · Pro Tip
🛏️
Bedroom

Freshen a Mattress With Baking Soda

Strip the bed and sprinkle a generous layer of baking soda over the entire mattress. Let it sit for at least 1 hour — longer if possible. Vacuum it up thoroughly. The baking soda absorbs moisture, odors, and dead skin cells, leaving your mattress fresh without any sprays or chemicals.

MU
Michel Uzhca · Pro Tip
🐾
Pet Owners

Remove Pet Hair From Furniture Instantly

Put on a rubber glove and run your hand across upholstery in one direction — the static lifts pet hair into clumps you can grab easily. A damp rubber squeegee works on carpet too. For stubborn hair in corners, wrap duct tape (sticky side out) around a paint roller for a budget-friendly lint roller.

EL
Evelyn Loja · Pro Tip
